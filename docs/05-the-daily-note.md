# The daily note

One page per day, and it is the only page you actually live in.

Everything else in the vault is a record you consult. The daily note is the surface: the agent writes into the top of it before you wake and the bottom of it after you close out, and you write in the middle all day.

```
Dailies/2026/07-July/2026-07-16.md
```

## The shape

```markdown
---
type: Note
subtype: daily-log
date: 2026-07-16
---
> {a quote, linked}

## `Morning Brief`
{written by the overnight routine}

### Launch
- [ ] Start the day at Inbox Zero
- [ ] Review today's priorities
- [ ] Clear the morning's reminders

{open space: your contemporaneous notes}

## `Evening Recap`
{written by the close-out ritual}

### Land
- [ ] End the day at Inbox Zero
- [ ] Set tomorrow's priorities
- [ ] Clear the evening's reminders
- [ ] Log the day's trackers
```

Read it top to bottom and it is a day: what happened yesterday, what's coming, how to start, what you thought, what got done, how to stop.

## Who owns what

| Part | Owner |
|---|---|
| The quote line | Agent picks it, from your collection |
| `## Morning Brief` | Agent (overnight routine) |
| `### Launch` | You. The agent never checks your boxes. |
| The open middle | You |
| `## Evening Recap` | Agent (close-out ritual) |
| `### Land` | You |

The agent writes the summaries. You do the work and the thinking. Neither touches the other's section.

## The brief has hard caps, and they are the point

Exactly two sections. About a hundred words. That's it.

- **Yesterday.** At most two bullets, and only what *materially* happened: a decision, a deliverable, an outcome. Not a log of the day. On a quiet day the section is omitted entirely.
- **Today.** Each meeting as a one-line link to its prepped note, one line for what's due, and a line or two for what actually matters most.

No Top 3. No open-loops section. No trajectory. No per-meeting paragraphs. No task dump.

The caps exist because an unconstrained brief becomes a wall of text you skim for a week and then stop opening, and a brief nobody reads is worse than no brief: it costs tokens and gives you the *feeling* of being prepared without the fact of it.

If something feels important enough to warrant a new section, it goes in the Today lines instead. The constraint forces the agent to decide what matters, which is the actual work.

The recap mirrors it: **Done** (one-line outcomes from every source, closing with an honest line about how the day went) and **Carrying to tomorrow** (open loops). Same discipline.

## Why the quote

The first line of every day is a quote from a collection you built, linked to its own note, picked by the agent and rotated so it doesn't repeat.

It is the least functional thing in the system and I would not remove it. It costs nothing, it takes one line, and it means the day starts with an idea instead of a task list. Systems people abandon are usually the ones with no warmth in them.

## The daily note closes the loop

This page is where the loop meets itself.

The overnight routine writes the brief into it. You work the day. The close-out ritual writes the recap into it and **commits**. Tonight's overnight run pulls that commit, so it enriches from a day that was actually closed out, which makes tomorrow's brief accurate, which is why you keep opening it.

Break any link and the whole thing degrades into notes nobody reads. The daily note is where you'd notice first.
